### 4.2 NurseJet

NurseJet is a nursing research and clinical briefing platform.

Core idea:

> NurseJet is a daily clinical briefing for bedside nurses. It distills new nursing research, guideline changes, and safety alerts into short briefs organized by specialty, with source citations and bedside takeaways.

NurseJet is designed around:

- Nursing research
- Clinical updates
- Safety alerts
- Guideline changes
- Specialty-specific units
- Exact citations
- Bedside takeaways
- Personalized “My Units”
- Top stories
- Admin daily edition workflow

The product direction is similar to a Perplexity Discover-style clinical feed, but focused for nurses and bedside care.

It should help nurses quickly understand what matters without digging through scattered journals, hospital emails, and policy updates.

Jethro built NurseJet because nurses lose time to scattered information, and research often does not reach bedside workflows in a clean, usable format.

Good answer to “What is NurseJet?”:

> NurseJet is a nursing-focused clinical briefing platform. It turns research updates, guideline changes, and safety alerts into short, source-cited briefs organized by unit or specialty. The idea is to give nurses the useful takeaway quickly, without making them dig through scattered sources.

Good answer to “Why did Jethro build NurseJet?”:

> Jethro is training in nursing and sees how much friction exists between research, guidelines, and bedside practice. NurseJet is his attempt to make clinical knowledge feel easier to access, more personalized, and more useful during real nursing work.

Important caution:

NurseJet should not present itself as a substitute for hospital policy, physician judgment, or clinical decision-making. It is a briefing and education tool.

Jethro believes AI in healthcare should subtract friction, not add another system.

Strong statement:

> I believe the future of healthcare should use AI to subtract friction, not add another system to fight.

Jethro is interested in AI that:

- Saves time
- Helps organize messy information
- Drafts documentation
- Surfaces what matters
- Helps people find source-backed answers
- Supports judgment instead of replacing it
- Gives clinicians and researchers back attention

For nursing specifically:

> Nurses lose hours of every shift to scattered tools, charting, and digging for information that should already be in front of them. AI should give that time back.

For medicine / healthcare broadly:

> The point is not smarter software for its own sake. The point is giving people in healthcare more time and attention for the patient, the experiment, or the decision in front of them.

Good answer to “What does Jethro believe the future should look like?”:

> Jethro wants a future where AI removes friction from healthcare and research. A nurse should be able to get source-backed information quickly. A researcher should be able to capture messy experiment notes without losing context. Documentation should feel less like fighting software and more like preserving what happened. The goal is not replacing judgment; it is giving people more time and attention for the work that matters.
